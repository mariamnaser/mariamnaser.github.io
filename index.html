Hi, I am Mariam Naser, a senior at Barnard College, Columbia University, majoring in Computer Science and Philosophy. Prior to college, I attended the Academy for Software Engineering in New York, NY. I have a strong interest in developing technologies that enhance society, helping people live richer, more fulfilling lives. Over the years, I have cultivated a diverse skill set in Rock Climbing, Finance, Philosophy, and Computer Science, enabling me to address problems through different perspectives and methodologies, even in high-stress situations while mitigating risks. My goal is to create or contribute to technologies that foster community engagement, helping users connect rather than isolate. I strive to design systems that ensure ease of use, educate, and engage users, promoting community interaction through technology or other means. Thank you for visiting my site, and I look forward to connecting with you!

As a member of Women in Computer Science (WiCS), a mentor in Kinship Climbing Collective, and an avid climber, I balance my technical pursuits with community engagement. I have a love for languages, being fluent in Arabic and continually deepening my knowledge of it, which bridges my cultural heritage with my professional aspirations. I am a in the beginning with learning Spanish, French, and Turkish.
